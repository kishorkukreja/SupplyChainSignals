THE MECHANISM

Inventory has always had more than one job

In a stable cycle, those jobs align. In a messy cycle, they split. A tariff deadline, a freight GRI, a slow supplier — each one can justify stock that finance wants eliminated.

JOB 1
Protect service
Buffer against stockouts. Keeps shelves full when lead times slip.
JOB 2
Protect price
Buy before a tariff, a GRI, or an input cost move. Lock in today's landed cost.
JOB 3
Buy time
Hedge against a slow supplier, tight vessel, or a policy clock you do not control.
THE PROBLEM
All three at once
When every clock moves together, inventory accumulates and reason codes blur.

THE REFRAME

Every unit makes a claim on the future

Treat inventory as a portfolio of bets, not a quantity to minimise. Each unit says something different:

1
We expect demand
Normal safety stock — confirm against orders
2
We fear a tariff
Policy hedge — needs a sell-through trigger and stop-buy date
3
We distrust a supplier
Reliability buffer — review when lead times normalise
4
We bought before a rate increase
Freight hedge — only rational if spot rates actually rose
5
We had no better choice
Old forecast — highest risk, most likely to become a markdown

HISTORICAL RHYMES

We have seen pieces of this before

2020–22 BULLWHIP

Orders made sense when placed. By the time they arrived, demand had shifted. Warehouses filled with stock that solved yesterday's problem.

2018–19 TARIFF PULL-FORWARD

Strong import months did not mean stronger consumption. A policy deadline was moving orders, not end demand. The signal was muddied.

2009–11 SYNCHRONIZED RESTOCK

When everyone cuts at once, suppliers feel a collapse. When everyone rebuilds at once, they feel a boom. The real end market was less dramatic than the order book.

This week has pieces of all three.

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT

Three paths from here

1
Soft landing

Demand holds. GRIs fade. Tariff details clarify. May inventory converts to sales without discounting. 62.5 looks prudent in hindsight.

WATCH: stable new orders + falling delivery indexes + lower spot rates
2
Bullwhip hangover

Front-loaded demand fades. Companies discover they bought ahead of customers. Inventory ages, markdowns rise. Sell-in and sell-through diverge.

WATCH: weaker June/July orders + rising DIO + higher storage costs
3
Policy and freight squeeze

Uncertainty persists. GRIs stick. Firms without clean master data cannot separate protected from exposed stock. Margin pressure shows before revenue weakness.

WATCH: July 6 tariff comments + emergency routing + S&OP exceptions
